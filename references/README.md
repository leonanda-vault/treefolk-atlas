# i-Tree SEA Offline Reference Library

This directory contains the official scientific documents and methodological references that underpin the **i-Tree SEA** ecosystem benefits engine.

## Reference Documents

### 1. i-Tree Core Methodology & Science
*   **[Understanding i-Tree: 2023 Summary of Programs and Methods](Understanding_iTree_2023_Methods.pdf)** (2.50 MB)
    *   *Author:* David J. Nowak (USDA Forest Service)
    *   *Significance:* The primary overview of i-Tree programs, detailing definitions, assumptions, core models, and data requirements for urban forest carbon, stormwater, and pollution calculations.
*   **[i-Tree Eco v6 Pollutant Removal, Biogenic Emissions, and Hydrologic Estimates](i-Tree_v6_Pollutant_Removal_and_Hydrologic_Estimates.pdf)** (4.03 MB)
    *   *Author:* i-Tree Team / USDA Forest Service
    *   *Significance:* The foundational mathematical reference for environmental models (pollution removal rates, water interception, run-off mitigation).
*   **[i-Tree Science Change Log (September 2021 Update)](i-Tree_Science_Change_Log_2021.pdf)** (0.41 MB)
    *   *Author:* i-Tree Research Group
    *   *Significance:* Details the transition to newer equations, specifically the implementation of the pantropical biomass formulas (Chave) and species-specific wood density weighting.

### 2. Implementation Guides & Model Details
*   **[i-Tree Eco v6 Guide for International Projects](i-Tree_Eco_v6_International_Projects_Guide.pdf)** (0.46 MB)
    *   *Author:* i-Tree Support Team
    *   *Significance:* Outlines requirements, proxies, and configurations necessary to apply i-Tree Eco outside the United States, including international climate and species mappings.
*   **[i-Tree Design v6 Methods](i-Tree_Design_v6_Methods.pdf)** (0.03 MB)
    *   *Author:* i-Tree Research Group
    *   *Significance:* Explains the mathematical formulations for individual tree calculations of carbon storage, annual sequestration, and hydrology.

### 3. Key Ecological Publications
*   **[Tree Height Integrated into Pantropical Forest Biomass Estimates (2012)](Feldpausch_2012_Tree_Height_Biomass.pdf)** (2.40 MB)
    *   *Author:* T. R. Feldpausch et al.
    *   *Significance:* Establishes height–diameter relationships and details the critical role tree height integration plays in reducing uncertainty in tropical forest biomass equations.
